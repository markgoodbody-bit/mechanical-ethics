1. **Voice — reclaimed.** "You wake up as someone." "Take a normal sort of case. Nothing dramatic. No science fiction. No killer robot. Just a letter." "I have worked in systems long enough to know that you need categories." The "I" is back and load-bearing, the rhythm breathes, there are real flowing paragraphs. The fix landed. Residual tic, and it's the last one: the **synonym-piles**. 2. **The rent-clock scene — works, and it's the best thing in the chapter.** "The system has a process. She has a clock." crystallises the entire middle-out point in six words, through the case. That's show-don't-tell, earned, not manipulative (you explicitly refuse melodrama). One flag: it's the *same case-class as the foreword* (benefit stopped, rent). Handled differently enough here (slower, one woman, the process/clock turn) that it's fine — but the welfare/benefit example is now your go-to in two consecutive pieces. Don't let it become your *only* example; a reader clocks when a thinker has one case.
3. **Middle-out now emerges, mostly.** "From the top it may look like policy. From the bottom, data. In the middle, someone trying to keep a life from narrowing before anyone with power has looked." It's attached to the woman now, not a free-floating diagram. Residual: a few leftover announcements ("This is why I keep coming back to the middle," "That is the place Mechanical Ethics begins"). Minor; trim a couple.
4. **"Something is here" — fixed.** Down to ~3 uses, and the closing passage *examines* it ("Not 'this is a person.' Not 'this has rights.' Just: do not erase the affected centre before you have looked") so it carries content, not mood. No longer an incantation.
5. **AI personhood — clean, and better than v0.1.** Both shortcuts refused; the down/outward/sideways move stops it being AI-special-pleading. No break.
6. **Argument vs atmosphere — enough for Chapter 1, with a forward warning.** There's a real spine now (start-too-high → the middle → process-vs-clock → categories aren't the person → AI treats patterns as world → act under partial sight without faking certainty → late correction ≠ working correction → harm travels through diffusion → don't start with blame). It moves; each step earns the next. But the whole chapter is *orientation* — "here is where we begin and why." That's legitimate for Chapter 1. The risk is forward: by the end the reader has a *posture*, not a *proven, contestable claim they could have resisted*. If Chapter 2 is also "here's another thing to notice," the book stays atmospheric. Ch2 must shift from orienting to *arguing* — take one claim someone could disagree with and defend it hard.
