Good — you went to Chapter 1. Reading it.

Read it. Straight, no softening: the **argument holds and the voice has collapsed**, and for this book of all books, that second thing is close to fatal. Let me show you exactly where.

1. **Middle-out starting point — established, yes.** The top/bottom/middle architecture is clear and it lands. Substance is sound. But it's established by *announcing* it — "The moral situation begins in the middle," "That is the middle," "That is where Mechanical Ethics starts" — exposition of a diagram, not a realization the reader lives through. Clear, but told.

2. **"Something is here" — the idea works, the phrase inflates.** The idea (refuse to let the affected thing disappear before the question starts) is the chapter's real contribution. But you repeat the bare phrase as an isolated line ~eight times until it stops being a claim and becomes an incantation. By the fifth "Something is here." a sceptical reader thinks: *you keep chanting this at me.* It's doing mood work, not argument work. That's the vague moral inflation the phrase risks, realised.

3. **AI personhood — handled well, no break.** "An AI is not a person just because it speaks in sentences"; "we have to ask whether something is happening there too"; "it may not even be clear yet what kind of thing it is." Open without granting status. This is the best-managed part. Leave it.

4. **Voice — this is the failure, and it's the big one.** The foreword was Mark: a living room, "I was talking," evenings, a body in a chair. This chapter has shed almost all of that and become a smooth, balanced, impersonal essay *about* his idea. The tells are everywhere and they're specifically the AI signature:
   - **Relentless parallel enumerations.** "body, language, memory, fear, training, culture, interface, data, attention." "move, choose, refuse, speak, hide, help, harm, obey, resist, build, destroy." "A person becomes a case number. A family becomes an output. A patient becomes a queue position. A worker becomes a score…" Nearly every paragraph piles up a parallel list for cadence. This is the clearest *this-was-AI-shaped* signal there is.
   - **The reflexive balanced concession.** Every claim is instantly softened: "There is value in that. I am not dismissing it." "Not because abstraction is always wrong. We need abstraction." A human with conviction lets some claims stand sharp; this never does.
   - **Unbroken one-line-gong rhythm.** It never relaxes into a normal flowing paragraph; every sentence strikes a bell. Sustained over a whole chapter it reads as performing profundity.
   The result: it sounds like a very capable AI wrote a philosophy essay about Mark's idea. **And this is the one book that cannot sound AI-written** — its entire credibility rests on "a human found this, the machine was a foil; the heart of the work is human." If the prose reads machine-generated, the reader concludes the human voice was a pose and the machine wrote the philosophy. That collapses the premise. In 2026 readers are primed to smell AI prose, and this trips every sensor.

5. **Concrete life — too abstract, and fake-concrete.** The foreword had one developed wound you could feel (rent gone, future narrowed, a person in it). This chapter has *lists of nouns* dressed as concreteness — "a worker becomes a score," "a patient becomes a queue position" — flashing past with no one inside them. There's no one to feel for. One followed-through scene would beat all ten lists.

6. **What stops a reader:** the prose reading as AI-generated (existential here); the "something is here" chant; the parade of lists with no person in them; the unrelieved gravity.
