# Claude Review — Mechanical Ethics Chapter 3 v0.1

Status: Claude review returned by Mark, 2026-06-30.

Decision: Chapter 3 holds and can move forward, but needs a cheap tonal/detail repair. Chapter 4 should show the framework straining rather than winning.

---

Read it. This is the best argument chapter yet — you actually staged the opponent, narrowed cleanly, and the AI-routing section is genuinely sharp. Attacking what still fails:

1. **Contestable claim, opponent staged — real progress, but it still wins too cleanly.** You took the note: "A real opponent to my argument would say… You cannot treat delay as action every time someone has a concern… That objection is not stupid. It is necessary." Good. But you stage the opponent and then *absorb* it rather than let it cost you anything — you pivot to "what is the concern doing to the clock," and your own distinction (good waiting vs bad waiting) dissolves the tension. The opponent lands a glancing blow (you admit paralysis is real), not a real one. **The hit you don't take:** your framework judges responsibility well *in retrospect* — "did you have a live role, could your silence affect visibility, was the clock closing faster than correction" — but those are backward-looking. They don't tell the engineer, *at decision time, with a weak signal*, whether THIS concern crosses the line. The opponent's strongest move is: "you've made me responsible either way — guilty of silence if I wait, guilty of crying wolf if I escalate — without giving me any way to decide which I'm doing." You leave that unanswered. That's the round the opponent should win, or you should concede.

2. **Narrowed enough — arguably over-narrowed.** The boundary (live role / materially affect / clock vs correction) genuinely prevents the inflation. But you raise the "this could be too broad" worry four times ("Not infinite weight. Not automatic guilt. Weight"; "If every delay is action then everything is guilt"; "I am aware this can become too broad"). Twice does it. The rest is anxious armour eating momentum.

3. **Opponent lands harder than Ch2's, not hard enough.** It's articulated and dignified ("paralysis wearing moral clothing" is a good line *for* the opponent). But it makes the general case for thresholds and you answer it; it never makes the specific case that your framework can't adjudicate the live call. Let it.

4. **Engineer case — right domain, but it's the least vivid of your three.** "No one is standing in a dark corridor holding a folder. Just someone doing their job" is good, voice-y, de-dramatising. But it stays a *category*: "a system," "a problem," "a small number of people," "a particular condition." Compare Ch1's woman, a letter, rent due Friday. The engineer case is *typed*, not *peopled* — no specific product, no specific harm, no one on the receiving end — and it has no scene-moment, it just dissolves into the argument. One concrete detail (what the system does, who gets hurt how) would make it a situation instead of a slot.

5. **Voice — the AI-enumeration is mostly fixed, but the *portentous* tic has taken over.** A few synonym-piles remain ("rank, filter, recommend, prioritise, suppress, flag, or route"). The bigger issue is gong-density: "The world has moved." "Weight." "Especially then." "The missed brake is part of the harm." "If the future closes, something acted." And the ending — "That is not comfortable. It is not meant to be. It is the price of admitting that time is real." — is three gongs in a row reaching for gravitas. You fixed the machine-essay rhythm; the every-sentence-a-revelation rhythm is now the dominant residual, heavier than Ch1 v0.2.

6. **AI routing bridge — works, doesn't swallow the chapter, and it's your best new material.** "They act when they rank, filter, recommend… A list is reordered. A warning is hidden… the world has moved. Someone's chance of being seen has changed." "'the AI only advised' is not always enough." Sharp, late-placed, a real extension of the claim. Keep all of it.

7. **What stops a reader:** the portentous close (eye-roll risk); the opponent granted dignity then quietly absorbed; the engineer case staying abstract; the over-reassurance.
