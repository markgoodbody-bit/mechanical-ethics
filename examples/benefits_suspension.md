# Worked Example: Benefits Suspension or Recovery

## Scenario

A public body suspends benefit payments or begins recovery after a fraud flag, overpayment decision, automated match, or administrative review.

The affected person can appeal or request review, but the money stops or recovery continues while the route runs.

## 1. Selection

The system selects a claimant for suspension, recovery, investigation, or altered payment.

The selection may come from:

- automated data matching;
- risk scoring;
- internal review;
- caseworker decision;
- policy rule;
- debt-recovery process.

## 2. Affected person

The claimant carries the consequence.

Possible effects:

- income loss;
- debt recovery;
- rent arrears;
- food and utility pressure;
- stress and illness;
- reduced ability to gather evidence;
- pressure to accept an incorrect decision because challenge takes too long.

## 3. Hardening point

Hardening may occur before the legal process ends.

Examples:

- rent due before appeal;
- debt recovered before dispute resolves;
- account transferred to enforcement;
- claimant borrows money or misses essentials;
- evidence becomes harder to retrieve;
- stress and practical harm accumulate.

## 4. Correction carrier

Possible carriers:

- suspension pause during dispute;
- hardship payment;
- urgent review;
- independent tribunal;
- statutory deadline for response;
- affected-person notice;
- evidence disclosure;
- route to stop recovery while dispute is live.

A route that cannot pause suspension or recovery may be too late to protect the affected person.

## 5. Timing fit

The key comparison is not simply appeal time against statutory deadline.

The key comparison is appeal time against the claimant's practical hardening clock: rent, food, debt, health, childcare, transport, and survival pressure.

## 6. Classification

Likely result where payment stops or recovery continues during dispute:

**AFTER-THE-FACT REVIEW or FALSE SAFEGUARD**

Reason: the appeal may later correct the decision, but if the claimant absorbs serious hardship before correction, the safeguard did not fully protect the live interest.

## 7. Minimum repair

A stronger route would include:

- automatic pause of disputed recovery where credible challenge is lodged;
- hardship-safe interim state;
- clear notice and evidence disclosure;
- rapid first review;
- independent escalation;
- deadline for the corrector, not only the claimant;
- record of whether the claimant was restored, not only whether the case was closed.

## One-sentence result

A benefits appeal is not live protection if the claimant carries rent, food, debt, or enforcement pressure before the appeal can realistically change the path.
