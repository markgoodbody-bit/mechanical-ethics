# Worked Example: Hiring Rejection After Role Filled

This is a stylised example designed to show a clear **AFTER-THE-FACT REVIEW** result.

## Scenario

An organisation uses an automated screening system to reject applicants before interview.

Rejected applicants can request review, but review normally takes 30 days.

The shortlist closes in 7 days and the role is usually filled within 21 days.

## 1. Selection

The system selects applicants for rejection before interview.

It may be officially advisory, but downstream staff normally accept the rejection list.

## 2. Affected person

Rejected applicants carry the consequence.

Possible effects:

- lost job opportunity;
- lost income;
- career delay;
- inability to challenge because the reason is unclear;
- no restoration if the role is filled.

## 3. Hardening point

The consequence hardens when the shortlist closes and then again when the role is filled.

After the role is filled, the original opportunity is usually not restorable.

## 4. Correction carrier

The formal route is a review request.

It has some value:

- it may correct the applicant's record;
- it may identify a screening defect;
- it may improve future recruitment;
- it may support apology or compensation in some cases.

But it does not pause recruitment, does not reserve a place, and does not guarantee review before the shortlist closes.

## 5. Timing fit

Review time is about 30 days.

Shortlist hardening begins around 7 days.

The role is usually filled within 21 days.

Correction therefore usually arrives after the original opportunity has hardened.

## Working result label

**AFTER-THE-FACT REVIEW**

## Why this is not false safeguard

The review route may still matter.

It can expose errors, correct records, improve future processes, and possibly support remedy.

It is not empty theatre.

## Why this is not live protection

The review route usually cannot change the rejected applicant's path before the shortlist closes or the role is filled.

It cannot reliably restore the original opportunity.

## Minimum repair

To become closer to live protection, the system would need:

- notice of automated screening;
- actionable reason for rejection;
- rapid review inside the shortlist window;
- reserved interview slot or pause mechanism for credible challenges;
- reviewer with authority to reinstate the applicant before interviews conclude;
- record of false negatives, not only selected candidates.

## One-sentence result

This safeguard appears to be after-the-fact review because the review route may expose and correct errors later, but it usually cannot restore the applicant to the live recruitment process before the role is filled.
