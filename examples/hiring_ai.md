# Worked Example: AI Hiring Screen

## Scenario

An organisation uses an AI system to score job applications and recommend which candidates should receive interviews. Human staff usually accept the recommendations unless something looks obviously wrong.

## 1. Selection

The system ranks applicants and recommends who should proceed to interview.

The selection may be officially advisory, but if staff usually follow the recommendation, it may be practically binding.

## 2. Affected person

Rejected applicants carry the consequence.

Possible effects:

- lost job opportunity;
- lost income;
- reduced career path;
- opaque rejection;
- reduced ability to challenge because the applicant may never know AI was involved.

## 3. Hardening point

The main hardening point is when interviews are completed and the role is filled.

After that, a successful challenge may correct the record or improve future process, but it probably cannot restore the original opportunity.

## 4. Correction carrier

Possible carriers:

- notice that AI screening was used;
- explanation of the rejection basis;
- human review on request;
- pause before the shortlist is closed;
- audit of false negatives;
- ability to reinstate an applicant before interview decisions are final.

A generic complaints route after recruitment closes is weak protection.

## 5. Timing fit

If review takes longer than the hiring window, it is not live protection for the applicant.

## 6. Classification

Likely result without more safeguards:

**PARTIAL PROTECTION or AFTER-THE-FACT REVIEW**

Reason: the organisation may have human oversight or audit, but unless applicants can trigger review before shortlisting and hiring decisions harden, the safeguard does not protect the lost opportunity in time.

## 7. Minimum repair

A stronger route would include:

- notice that AI was used;
- a short window to request review;
- a human reviewer with authority to restore the applicant to the shortlist;
- a pause or reserve mechanism before final interview closure;
- records showing why the applicant was screened out;
- periodic review of rejected candidates, not just accepted candidates.

## One-sentence result

The AI hiring safeguard is only live protection if a wrongly screened applicant can discover, challenge, and reverse the rejection before the shortlist or job decision closes.
