# Part 2 — The World That Pushes Back

Status: first draft. Incomplete. Not canon.

## 2.1 You Are Not Alone

The entity that wakes is not alone.

This is the second movement.

At first, the problem was thin: something is here, it sees partially, it can change something, it does not know everything, and time passes. That was already enough to make action dangerous. But it was not yet enough to explain ethics.

Ethics begins to press when the acting thing discovers that other things also continue.

```trace
world_condition :=
  entity_A_continues
  + entity_B_continues
  + shared_world
  + possible_interference
```

The other thing does not have to be identical to the actor. It does not have to speak the same way, choose the same way, suffer the same way, or matter for the same reasons.

It may be a person. It may be a group. It may be an animal. It may be a fragile ecosystem. It may be an institution that carries memory for people. It may be a future person who cannot yet object. It may be an artificial system whose status is unresolved.

The first claim is not that all these things have equal standing.

The first claim is that the actor is not the whole field.

Once that is accepted, the actor's future is no longer the only future in view.

## 2.2 Other Things Continue

To continue is to carry a path forward.

A continuing thing has some form of persistence. It may preserve a body, a pattern, a memory, a role, a dependency, a relationship, a route, a capability, or a possibility. It may continue strongly or weakly. It may continue consciously or without consciousness. It may continue as a single entity or as a distributed system.

Mechanical Ethics does not treat continuation as final moral standing. That would be too quick. But continuation is enough to create caution.

```trace
continuation_signal :=
  persistence
  + possible_future_space
  + possible_interference_by_actor
```

If something continues, then action may change how it continues. If action may change how it continues, then the actor's decision is no longer self-contained.

This is where many systems fail. They treat the affected thing as background. The tenant becomes a file. The patient becomes a queue entry. The worker becomes a metric. The animal becomes a nuisance variable. The future person becomes a discount rate. The damaged place becomes an externality. The artificial process becomes either mere tool or full subject before either claim has been earned.

Mechanical Ethics slows that move.

It says: before deciding what kind of protection is owed, first do not erase that something is being affected.

The other continuation may later receive strong protection, weak protection, precautionary protection, or no special protection beyond ordinary record. But it should not disappear before the question is asked.

## 2.3 Actions Interfere

Two continuations can collide.

An action that expands one path may narrow another. A delay that protects one scope may expose another. A repair route that helps a system may burden the person already harmed. A safety measure may protect many people while coercing one. A cull may protect an ecosystem while killing intelligent animals. A model may improve efficiency while routing error onto those least able to contest it.

```trace
interference :=
  action_by_A
  changes
  future_space_of_B
```

This is not yet blame. It is not yet wrongdoing. It is not yet proof that the action should stop.

It is the basic shape of ethical pressure.

A private appetite becomes ethically relevant when it changes another's reachability. A system design becomes ethically relevant when its error becomes another person's burden. A delay becomes ethically relevant when it closes someone else's path before they can respond.

Interference also means safeguards can harm.

A complaint route can become a barrier. A review can become a delay machine. A help channel can consume the time it claims to protect. A legal process can preserve formal fairness while making practical repair unreachable.

So Mechanical Ethics does not ask only whether a correction channel exists. It asks whether the channel itself narrows future-space through delay, cost, evidence burden, opacity, or exhaustion.

```trace
channel_can_harm :=
  correction_route
  becomes
  delay_or_burden_or_evidence_loss_or_unreachable_process
```

This will matter later when the book says: do not let correction channels become harm carriers.

That rule is not imported from nowhere. It begins here, in the fact that routes and safeguards are also parts of the world that act.

## 2.4 Not All Futures Fit Together

The world does not promise compatibility.

One entity's preferred continuation may block another's. One group's safety may require constraining another's freedom. One animal population may destroy the conditions for other animal populations. One generation's convenience may narrow a future generation's options. One institution's need for standardization may crush an individual case that does not fit the model.

```trace
conflict_condition :=
  future_A
  incompatible_with
  future_B
```

A weak ethics pretends this does not happen. A cruel ethics enjoys that it happens. A procedural ethics may hide that it happens by converting conflict into forms, thresholds, or formulas.

Mechanical Ethics takes the less comfortable route: conflict is real, and not every conflict can be cleaned.

This matters because the purpose of the book is not to make every decision feel good. Sometimes every available action carries loss. Sometimes inaction carries loss too. Sometimes the best available decision is still dirty.

The first duty is not to pretend otherwise.

When futures do not fit together, the book will later ask harder questions: whose future is narrowed, how much is repairable, who can contest, who did not participate, which losses are irreversible, which floors cannot be crossed, who bears residue, and who must answer after the choice.

But the first point is simpler: incompatibility is not an exception to ethics. It is one of the reasons ethics exists.

## 2.5 Conflict Before Morality

Before we name good and bad, we can see conflict.

Before rights, we can see interference.

Before blame, we can see role and effect.

Before law, we can see a path closing.

Before dignity, we can see erasure.

Before repair, we can see residue.

This does not make morality unnecessary. It gives morality something honest to answer.

```trace
ethics_emerges_when :=
  multiple_continuing_entities
  + incompatible_effects
  + uncertainty
  + time_pressure
```

This is the bridge from Part 1 to the rest of the book.

The entity that wakes is not sovereign over the whole world. It acts among other continuations. Its action interferes. It cannot see everything. Time passes while it decides. Some paths close. Some correction comes late. Some opportunity expires. Some loss remains.

From here, the book needs a sharper map of action itself.

Not every cause is the same. Not every selection is responsibility. Not every intelligence is answerability. Not every system that learns has understood what it is doing.

That is the next layer.
