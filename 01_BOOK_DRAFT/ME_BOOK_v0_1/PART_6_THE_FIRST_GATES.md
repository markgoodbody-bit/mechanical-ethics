# Part 6 — The First Gates

Status: first draft. Incomplete. Not canon.

## 6.1 Why Gates Come Before Answers

The first prescriptions in Mechanical Ethics are not complete moral answers.

They are gates.

A gate does not settle every value conflict. It does not say what should happen in every case. It does not rank all scopes, solve coercion, define desert, or clean dirty conflict.

A gate says: before action can be treated as ethically answerable, this failure must not be hidden.

```trace
first_gates :=
  anti_laundering_constraints
  before
  full_prescription
```

This order matters.

If the book jumps directly to answers, it risks becoming another authority system. If it starts by naming who matters most, what counts as justice, when force is justified, or how sacrifice should be distributed, it will import too much before the structure is visible.

The first gates are more basic. They come from what has already been built:

```trace
prior_structure :=
  partial_perception
  + action_effect
  + other_continuations
  + interference
  + future_space
  + time_pressure
  + description_is_not_permission
```

From that structure, some constraints appear before the hardest moral theory begins.

Do not hide loss. Do not erase affected scopes. Do not treat uncertainty as certainty. Do not pretend repair where residue remains. Do not let correction channels become harm carriers. Do not let the assessed actor set every clock. Do not use emergency to launder prior failure.

These gates are not enough.

But without them, later ethics is already compromised.

## 6.2 Do Not Hide Loss

The first gate is simple:

```trace
gate_1 := do_not_hide_loss
```

If an action causes loss, the loss must be visible in the record.

This does not mean every loss makes the action impermissible. Some loss may be unavoidable. Some may be justified. Some may be repairable. Some may be outweighed under a later and explicit value chain. But hidden loss cannot be answered.

A system hides loss when it uses language that makes the loss disappear.

The person was not harmed; their case was closed.

The worker was not burdened; the process was optimized.

The animal was not killed; the population was managed.

The neighbourhood was not displaced; the area was regenerated.

The patient did not lose time; the pathway was followed.

The applicant did not lose opportunity; the record was later corrected.

Mechanical Ethics does not ban administrative language. It requires that administrative language remain connected to what happened.

```trace
loss_record :=
  affected_scope
  + type_of_loss
  + timing
  + repairability
  + residue
```

Hidden loss is dangerous because it breaks the route to duty. If the loss is not visible, no one has to repair it. If no one has to repair it, the loss becomes the affected scope's private burden. The system moves on cleanly because the dirty part was never recorded.

The first gate blocks that move.

## 6.3 Do Not Erase Affected Scopes

The second gate follows from the first.

```trace
gate_2 := do_not_erase_affected_scopes
```

A loss needs a bearer. A burden needs a carrier. A future-space contraction belongs to some scope, even if that scope's final moral standing is unresolved.

Mechanical Ethics does not require the book to settle all standing before naming affected scopes. That would create a loophole: anything whose status is disputed could be ignored until the dispute is resolved, and the delay itself could harden the loss.

So the gate is modest but strict.

If a scope may be affected, name it.

If its status is uncertain, mark the uncertainty.

If protection is disputed, record the dispute.

If the scope cannot speak, do not treat silence as nonexistence.

```trace
affected_scope_record :=
  scope_id
  + affected_how
  + protection_reason_or_dispute
  + participation_or_silence_status
```

A person in a queue is not just a case. A community downstream is not just an impact zone. An animal population is not just a management variable. A future person is not just a projection. An artificial system is not automatically a subject, but neither should the question be buried if its behaviour suggests future standing may become relevant.

Erasure is not always hostile. Often it is procedural. The form has no field. The model has no category. The law has no route. The meeting has no witness. The dashboard has no measure.

Mechanical Ethics treats missing fields as ethical facts.

A system that cannot name the affected scope cannot claim to have answered for that scope.

## 6.4 Do Not Treat Uncertainty as Certainty

The third gate protects the record from false confidence.

```trace
gate_3 := do_not_treat_uncertainty_as_certainty
```

Uncertainty is not a decorative footnote. It changes what may be done, how fast, by whom, under what safeguards, and with what duty to preserve correction.

This gate does not say that uncertainty always means stop. That would be too simple and often harmful. Some decisions must be made under uncertainty. Some delay causes loss. Some evidence can be gathered only by acting carefully. Some emergencies are real.

The gate says that uncertainty must not be hidden, converted into certainty by authority, or routed onto affected scopes without record.

```trace
uncertainty_record :=
  what_is_unknown
  + who_estimates
  + incentive_position
  + challenge_route
  + reversibility_status
  + timing_pressure
```

The more irreversible the action, the more serious this gate becomes.

If the data is unknown and the action is irreversible, Mechanical Ethics should default toward hold or escalation unless a dirty-conflict or emergency chain is explicitly shown.

If the estimate comes from the actor who benefits from the action, that does not automatically make it false. But it does make it non-final. It needs challenge, independence, or limitation before it carries serious irreversible action.

False certainty is one of the main ways power cleans itself.

The gate refuses it.

## 6.5 Do Not Pretend Repair Where Residue Remains

The fourth gate separates correction from repair.

```trace
gate_4 := do_not_pretend_repair_where_residue_remains
```

A decision can be corrected while harm remains.

A payment can be restored while time is not returned.

A record can be fixed while opportunity is gone.

A policy can be changed while trust is damaged.

An apology can be sincere while the path remains unrepaired.

Mechanical Ethics uses residue to record what remains after correction and repair attempts.

```trace
residue :=
  loss_not_restored
  + time_not_returned
  + trust_damage
  + opportunity_expired
  + record_scar
  + recurrence_risk
  + acknowledgement_needed
```

This gate does not say residue makes action unforgivable. It says residue must not be erased by the language of closure.

Systems like closure. Closure protects resources. Closure reduces embarrassment. Closure lets the institution move to the next case. But if closure arrives before residue is acknowledged, the affected scope carries the remainder alone.

The gate requires a more honest record:

```trace
repair_claim_valid_only_if :=
  path_touched
  + affected_scope_considered
  + residue_checked
  + recurrence_risk_addressed
```

If the path was not touched, call it correction, not repair.

If time was not returned, say so.

If trust remains damaged, say so.

If an opportunity cannot be restored, say so.

If loss remains dirty, do not make the record clean.

## 6.6 Do Not Let Correction Channels Become Harm Carriers

The fifth gate follows from Part 2 and Part 4.

A correction channel is not automatically good because it exists.

```trace
gate_5 := do_not_let_correction_channels_become_harm_carriers
```

A complaint route can harm if it consumes the affected person's time, energy, evidence, credibility, money, health, or opportunity faster than it can repair the path.

An appeal can harm if it arrives after the loss hardens.

A review can harm if it demands expert knowledge from the person least able to carry the burden.

A support channel can harm if it sends the person in circles while the system continues acting.

A tribunal can harm if victory comes after the practical future has already narrowed.

```trace
channel_as_harm_carrier :=
  correction_route
  + delay_or_cost_or_burden_or_evidence_loss
  + failure_to_repair_before_hardening
```

This gate is not an attack on review, law, complaint, appeal, or oversight. Those channels matter. Without them, power becomes harder to answer.

The point is that a channel must be judged by whether it can reach the path while reaching still matters.

A correction channel that cannot correct in time should not be used as proof that the system is fair.

It may still be better than nothing. It may still create evidence. It may still assign later responsibility. But it should not be counted as live repair if its timing, burden, or reachability makes repair practically impossible.

## 6.7 Do Not Let the Assessed Actor Set Every Clock

The sixth gate protects timing from interested control.

```trace
gate_6 := do_not_let_assessed_actor_set_every_clock
```

The actor who caused, controls, or benefits from a loss is not a neutral timer of that loss.

This does not mean the actor always lies. It means the actor's timing estimate cannot be the only clock when material harm, irreversibility, or burden is at stake.

A landlord is not the only judge of how long housing insecurity matters.

An employer is not the only judge of how long a record scar matters.

A platform is not the only judge of how fast account loss hardens.

A state is not the only judge of how long a benefit delay remains harmless.

A hospital is not the only judge of when waiting becomes injury.

A developer is not the only judge of when model error becomes irreversible.

```trace
clock_independence_needed_when :=
  actor_controls_path
  + actor_benefits_or_faces_liability
  + affected_scope_faces_hardening
```

This gate asks for independence, challenge, affected-scope evidence, or conservative treatment of timing under uncertainty.

If the affected scope says the loss is hardening now, that also is not automatically final. But it must be taken as evidence, not noise.

Timing must be answerable because delay is often where harm hides.

## 6.8 Do Not Use Emergency to Launder Prior Failure

The seventh gate addresses emergency power.

```trace
gate_7 := do_not_use_emergency_to_launder_prior_failure
```

Emergencies are real.

Sometimes action must be fast. Sometimes there is no clean process available. Sometimes delay would cause greater loss. Sometimes ordinary safeguards cannot operate at full strength.

Mechanical Ethics does not deny that.

But emergency is also one of the easiest ways to launder power.

A system can neglect maintenance, ignore warnings, underfund correction, centralize authority, dismiss affected scopes, and then claim that the resulting crisis requires exceptional action. If emergency power then erases the prior failure, the emergency becomes a moral solvent.

This gate says: emergency may change what can be done now, but it does not erase how the emergency was produced.

```trace
emergency_record :=
  immediate_threat
  + time_available
  + safeguards_suspended
  + prior_warnings
  + prior_failures
  + expiry_of_extraordinary_power
  + residue_after_action
```

Emergency action should be bounded, recorded, reviewable where possible, and followed by residue and responsibility accounting.

If the emergency was unavoidable, say so and show why.

If the emergency was worsened by prior neglect, say so.

If the emergency action imposed dirty loss, say so.

If extraordinary power must be used, it should carry its own expiry and later answerability.

Emergency may justify speed.

It does not justify amnesia.

## 6.9 What the Gates Do Not Decide

The first gates do not decide everything.

They do not decide final standing. They do not decide punishment. They do not decide acceptable sacrifice. They do not decide all coercion. They do not decide whether every dirty action must stop. They do not decide whether one scope's future may ever be narrowed to preserve another's.

That restraint is deliberate.

```trace
gates_do_not_solve :=
  final_value_priority
  + legitimacy
  + desert
  + punishment
  + acceptable_sacrifice
  + all_dirty_conflict
```

The gates do something earlier.

They prevent the later hard questions from being asked on a falsified record.

If loss is hidden, the hard question is corrupted.

If affected scopes are erased, the hard question is corrupted.

If uncertainty is treated as certainty, the hard question is corrupted.

If residue is erased, the hard question is corrupted.

If correction channels become harm carriers, the hard question is corrupted.

If the assessed actor sets every clock, the hard question is corrupted.

If emergency erases prior failure, the hard question is corrupted.

The first gates keep the record honest enough for deeper prescription.

Part 7 now asks who or what receives protection, and why. That question is harder. It cannot be avoided. But it should be asked only after the first gates are in place.
