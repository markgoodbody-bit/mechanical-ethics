# Glossary

Short definitions for terms used in the Correction Window Test.

These are working terms for the diagnostic. They are not a theory.

## Actual fix mechanism

The thing that can actually stop, pause, reverse, repair, compensate, or prevent recurrence.

A policy, dashboard, warning, or advisory board is not enough unless it has authority to change the outcome in time.

## Affected person

The person or group whose money, time, access, status, safety, care, housing, work, future options, or practical burden changes because of the system.

## After-the-fact review

A route that can expose failure, correct records, compensate, sanction, or prevent recurrence, but usually cannot change the original consequence before it is too late.

## Correction

An action that can stop, pause, reverse, repair, compensate, or prevent recurrence after an error is detected.

## Correction window

The time between a possible harmful decision and the too-late point.

## Decision

What the system does, ranks, recommends, approves, refuses, classifies, flags, routes, triggers, or changes.

A decision can be officially advisory but practically binding if people or other systems usually follow it.

## Evidence

The record that lets someone show what happened.

Useful evidence may include inputs, outputs, reasons, timestamps, notices, challenge routes, human-review records, audit trails, override logs, and repair outcomes.

## False safeguard

A safeguard that exists in wording, policy, dashboard, audit, form, or public claim but cannot meaningfully alter the affected person's path in time.

## Human oversight

Human involvement in a decision route.

Human oversight is not automatically protection. It matters only if the human has time, evidence, authority, incentives, and permission to intervene before it is too late.

## Live protection

A safeguard that can still change the affected person's path before the too-late point.

## Partial protection

A safeguard that has some real protective features, but one or more of timing, authority, evidence, access, or resources is incomplete.

## Too-late point

The point where ordinary correction no longer restores the original path.

Examples:

- job filled;
- benefit stopped;
- rent missed;
- medical window closed;
- account closed;
- output copied downstream;
- data copied downstream;
- evidence deleted;
- debt recovered;
- deadline passed.

## Unknown

A valid result where public evidence does not show enough to classify the safeguard.

Unknown should not be converted into safety, failure, or reassurance.

## Working result label

A judgement prompt used by the test.

The labels are not validated instrument categories. They are a way to state the result clearly while preserving uncertainty.
