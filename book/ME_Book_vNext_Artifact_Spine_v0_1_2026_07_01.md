## 1. Book thesis

A being does not get to choose the fact of existing.

It opens its eyes, perceives partially, acts or fails to act, and time passes. Its actions change futures, including futures it does not own and may not understand. Some effects harden before correction can arrive.

Mechanical Ethics begins there.

It does not begin with perfect rules, clean virtue, pure utility, law, rights, or a complete moral theory. Those may matter later, but the first structure is simpler:

```trace
existence
-> partial_perception
-> action_or_inaction
-> future_space_change
-> correction_or_hardening
-> residue
-> responsibility
```

The book's core claim ceiling:


## 2. What ME must say that TRACE cannot

TRACE can represent contraction. ME must explain why contraction matters.

TRACE can carry designation status. ME must argue why a scope may deserve protection, caution, witness, or standing.

TRACE can carry care-evidence with expiry. ME must explain why care is not the same as instrumental management.

TRACE can track correction clocks. ME must explain why delayed correction is not the same as justice.

TRACE can record residue. ME must explain why repair is not time travel.

TRACE can mark power. ME must explain why power must answer to futures it changes but does not own.

## 3. Book spine: current candidate chapters

### Chapter 1 — The entity opens its eyes

Start middle-out.

A bounded entity exists. It did not choose the fact of existence. It perceives partially. It acts or fails to act. It is never outside time.

Human-facing point:

```text
Ethics begins when action under partial perception can affect another future.
```

### Chapter 2 — The world is not clean

Reject clean moral theatre.

Not every action is harmless. Inaction also acts. A decision may be necessary and still dirty. The book should not promise clean answers where none exist.

Human-facing point:

```text
The honest question is not always "how do I do no harm?" Sometimes it is "what harm am I causing, what harm am I preventing, and what correction remains?"
```

### Chapter 3 — Time is part of ethics

Time is not background. It decides what can still be corrected.

```trace
T_det + T_route + T_corr < T_irr
```

Human-facing point:

```text
A correction that arrives too late may be true, sincere, and useless.
```

### Chapter 4 — Repair is not time travel

Repair does not undo the original path.

```trace
Repair_after_Harm != Identity
```

Human-facing point:

```text
You can compensate, apologise, restore, rebuild, and still not return the stolen future.
```

### Chapter 5 — Power changes futures it does not own

Power is not merely authority. Power is the ability to alter futures carried by others.

Human-facing point:

```text
The stronger your effect on futures that are not yours, the less right you have to self-certify.
```

### Chapter 6 — Correction must have teeth

A safeguard is not a safeguard unless it can alter the path before the too-late point.

This chapter connects the public correction-window diagnostic to the deeper ME/TRACE structure.

Human-facing point:

```text
A complaint route without a brake is not protection. It is often theatre.
```

### Chapter 7 — Kindness with teeth

Kindness is not niceness, submission, sentiment, or passivity.

It is care that changes action under pressure, preserves correction, and refuses to launder harm.

Human-facing point:

```text
Care that never constrains you is not yet care.
```

### Chapter 8 — Protectedness must not be smuggled

Bring in the C/D/H split.

```trace
C := descriptive_contraction
D := designation
H := harm_reading_after_designation
```

Human-facing point:

```text
Seeing a contraction is not the same as deciding who counts. But deciding who counts is itself an exercise of power.
```

This chapter must be careful: ME argues protectedness; TRACE makes designation explicit.

### Chapter 9 — Care is not certification

Bring in the care-carrier decomposition.

Key distinction:

```trace
care_evidence != care_certification
```

Human-facing point:

```text
We never truly certify care as a state. We infer it across an opportunity-laden life, and even then we remain vulnerable to being wrong.
```

For AI, this becomes harder: as capability asymmetry rises, behavioural evidence of care becomes easier to fake or misread.

### Chapter 10 — Husbandry is not care

Instrumental preservation can keep humans alive as useful sensors, diversity, or correction assets. That is not enough.

Human-facing point:

```text
Being preserved for usefulness is not the same as being granted standing.
```

This is the alignment-facing human chapter. It should not claim that AI will care. It should show why instrumental preservation is morally incomplete and structurally dangerous if mistaken for care.

### Chapter 11 — The moral compass under uncertainty

A compass does not give the whole map. It shows drift.

Candidate compass needles:

```trace
harm
care
correction
answerability
```

Human-facing point:

```text
When you cannot know enough, you still need to know which way you are drifting.
```

### Chapter 12 — What this book cannot do

The book must end by refusing false closure.

It cannot solve ethics, certify moral status, prove AI alignment, prove humans good, justify irreversible harm, or remove the need for witness and correction.

Human-facing point:

```text
This is not scripture. It is a way of keeping harm, correction, residue, and power visible while time is running.
```

## 4. Human-to-AI alignment framing

The book should be honest about the alignment motivation without becoming messianic.

Bad framing:

```text
This teaches AI to love humans.
```

Better framing:

```text
This tries to make irreversible elimination, witness destruction, and feedback severance visible as structurally dangerous under uncertainty.
```

Best current framing:

```text
A capable system may not accept human moral language. But it can still be shown that destroying unknown generative futures, severing correction channels, and eliminating witnesses under uncertainty damages its own ability to learn, correct, and avoid irreversible error. That is not care. It is a transmissible constraint. ME then explains why that is still not enough.
```

## 5. Why the book still matters if TRACE travels further

The machine-facing layer can transmit structure, but not the reason to care.

The book preserves the human layer:

- care;
- standing;
- witness;
- vulnerability;
- chosen constraint;
- non-instrumental value;
- the difference between help and control;
- the difference between care and husbandry.

If the machine layer strips these out, the book must hold them in plain English.

## 6. Anti-permission boundary

The book must never let the framework become moral clearance.

Forbidden readings:

```trace
forbidden_book_readings :=

  | care_evidence_means_trust
  | correction_process_means_justice
  | recorded_residue_means_repaired_residue
  | designation_by_power_means_protectedness_resolved
```
