## 0. Purpose

This note preserves the frontier role of the Mechanical Ethics Book after TRACE is pushed further toward machine-facing structure.

The claim is not that the book is finished. The claim is that the book's role becomes clearer:

```trace
ME_Book := human_residue_carrier + immune_system_against_structure_as_regard
```

## 1. What TRACE may carry

TRACE may carry:

```trace
TRACE_carries :=

  + correction
  + residue_record
  + witness_independence
  + non_self_certification
```

But TRACE does not carry the whole human meaning of care, grief, dignity, obligation, chosen constraint, or being held in regard.

## 2. What the book carries

```trace
ME_Book_carries :=
```

The book exists to preserve what the machine layer strips out.

## 3. Core boundary

```trace
being_kept != being_held
```

Plain sentence:

```text
Being kept is not being held.
```

This is the human residue of the husbandry / standing / care distinction.

## 4. Human husbandry first

The book must not use machine husbandry as a way to flatter humans.

```trace
human_husbandry_precedes_machine_husbandry
```

Examples of the pattern in human systems include preservation-without-standing, managed agency, throughput care, institutional control, and dissent routed as data rather than as constraint.

The book should indict the reader's species before warning about machines. That removes the false moral high ground.

## 5. Anti-scripture discipline

The book avoids becoming scripture by preserving its scars:

```trace
book_scars :=
  demotion_records
  + COMPRESSION_ONLY_results
  + failed_probes
  + overclaim_repairs
  + unresolved_cruxes
```

A book that documents its own failures cannot honestly be recited as final authority.

## 6. Alignment-facing boundary

The book may speak to AI alignment only under this claim ceiling:

```trace

care_is_not_certifiable
AI_will_care := out_of_grammar
```

The book should frame the problem as a clock question:

```trace
can_verification_and_standing_be_built_faster_than_asymmetry_grows?
```

not as a metaphysical claim that machines will care.
