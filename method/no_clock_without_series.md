# No Clock Without a Series

## Rule

Do not make a public claim about a clock moving unless there is a series someone else can inspect and extend.

## Why

A single number can show context. It cannot show direction.

A one-year comparison can suggest pressure. It cannot establish long-term decline or recovery.

A vivid example can reveal a pattern. It cannot prove that the pattern is general.

## Examples

### Weak claim

Debt interest is much larger than law-courts spending in one year, therefore repair is being outbid.

Problem: this is a level comparison, not a change series. It may be useful context, but it does not prove outbidding over time.

### Stronger claim

Over fifteen years, law-courts spending as a share of total managed expenditure fell while debt-interest and defence shares rose.

This would still need caveats, but it is at least a series claim.

### Weak claim

A tribunal backlog is high, therefore correction capacity is collapsing.

Problem: backlog alone may reflect demand, capacity, policy change, case mix, or measurement changes.

### Stronger claim

Receipts, disposals, open caseload, measured clearance time, case mix, and capacity denominator are tracked together across a long series, and latency worsens after controls.

## Minimum series standard

A public clock should state:

- source;
- date range;
- unit;
- field definitions;
- measurement breaks;
- missing fields;
- update route;
- what would weaken the interpretation.

## Preferred wording

Use:

> This surface shows pressure in the available series, with caveats.

Avoid:

> This proves collapse.

Use:

> This is a segment clock.

Avoid:

> This is the full repair clock.

Use:

> This one-year ratio provides denominator context.

Avoid:

> This one-year ratio proves repair has been outbid.

## Public discipline

If there is no series, call it:

- example;
- context;
- hypothesis;
- prompt for measurement;
- not evidence of trend.

If there is a series, publish:

- raw data;
- source map;
- data dictionary;
- caveats;
- falsifiers;
- update instructions.

## Core sentence

Nothing is evidence until it is a series someone else can extend.
